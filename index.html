22

YEARS.

That's how long it's been since DePaul men's basketball played in the NCAA Tournament. The longest drought of any power-conference program in America.

DePaul's last NCAA Tournament win came in 2004. To put that in perspective: the iPhone didn't exist. Mark Aguirre and Rod Strickland were already retired from the NBA. The kids on this year's roster were in diapers — or hadn't been born yet.

Since DePaul joined the Big East in 2005–06, the Blue Demons have a conference record of roughly 22–265. They've won barely 20% of their league games. They've finished dead last in the Big East eleven times.

DeWayne Peevy was hired in August 2020 to fix it. Six seasons later, the drought is longer, not shorter.

"If we don't hurry up and take advantage of [the brand], there will be no generation who has seen that success."

— DeWayne Peevy, August 2020

He was right. He just didn't realize he'd be the AD presiding over its extinction.

A $175 Million Arena.
And No One Came.

Wintrust Arena seats 10,387. Built in part with taxpayer money. Designed to be a downtown showcase for DePaul basketball.

Under DeWayne Peevy, the Blue Demons have averaged around 4,045 fans per game — less than 40% capacity. Multiple games have drawn fewer than 1,000 turnstile entries. In the first six seasons at Wintrust, only one men's basketball game ever sold out. Even that one only hit 75% of capacity.

The student section? Routinely empty. The atmosphere? Funereal. The investment? Bleeding cash.

And His Reward
